Five Days of Fable 5 — Day 3: The Farm. 🌱 (Jeff's, not Clarkson's.)

We were going to lead with a Google Sheets extension. Useful? Sure. Exciting? About as thrilling as a spreadsheet sounds.

Then my friend Jeff Von Stetton called.

Jeff runs Holistics Unlimited, and he didn't want another off-the-shelf platform — he wanted something that actually belonged to his community. Regenerative agriculture, honest wellness brands, real people. He asked if I wanted to collaborate.

By the end of the call the shape was obvious: take the playbook that Twitter, X, and Threads all run on — a feed, posts, photos and video, real sign-in — and re-root it for Jeff's world.

Turns out I've done this before. I used to run production for Piczo, the social network for the MySpace-to-Facebook generation, founded by Jim Conning. Standing up a community platform is old muscle memory.

So I built it. I sent Jeff a working prototype before we hung up — not a mockup, an app he could click through while we were still on the line.

Then I deployed it to Vercel the same night.

No pitch deck. No six-month roadmap. A phone call to production in a matter of hours — with Fable 5 in the driver's seat.
